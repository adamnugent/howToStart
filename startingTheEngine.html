Now What?

Now that the aircraft is ready to start, you can begin the start sequence. After ensuring the throttle (circled in green)is in the full aft position, move the jet fuel starter switch (circled in red) to the "start 2" position. This will start a small jet engine which will in turn start the main engine.

Once the jet fuel starter engine is running, keep an eye on the engine speed (circled in blue) and the fan turbine inlet temperature (circled in red). If the fan turbine inlet temperature exceeds 1,100 degrees celcius, immediately retard the throttle to the "off" position. While the jet fuel starter is running, the engine speed will slowly begin to increase. Once it reaches 20%, lift the throttle upwards and advance it, then lower it and retard it until it stops. This is idle power. Shortly after this, the jet fuel starter will automatically stop and its switch will return to the neutral position.

As long as nothing catches fire, explodes, or otherwise goes wrong, you've successfully started your F-16! Move ahead to the next page for precautions regarding your now-running jet aircraft.
