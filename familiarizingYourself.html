Now What?

Here we have a picture of a typical F-16 cockpit as seen by the pilot. Before entering the seat, a word of warning: while the seat will typically not be armed except immediately prior to a flight, do NOT pull the yellow ejection handle located in front of the seat unless you literally want to die. To the right of the seat is the sidestick controller; this is used to control the roll and pitch of the aircraft in flight. Opposite the seat from the sidestick controller is the throttle. To advance the throttle from off to idle or from full power into augmenter mode, it must be lifted upwards. To retard the throttle from idle to off, it must be lifted and at the same time a lever at the base of the throttle must be depressed. The other controls and indicators are generally not useful for mereley starting the engine, but those which are important will be labeled and discussed later on.
